# Seth Godin's Marketing Principles — Applied to Kompete.ai

> A MECE framework synthesized across Godin's books and blog, then mapped to where Kompete.ai is today: what we're getting right, what's open, what to sharpen.
>
> Last updated: 2026-04-13

---

## The MECE Framework — 7 Principles

Each principle answers a different question, so together they form a non-overlapping, collectively-exhaustive view of marketing.

| # | Principle | Question it answers | Primary source |
|---|-----------|---------------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Purpose** | *Why* does this exist? What change do we seek to make? | *This Is Marketing*, *Song of Significance* |
| 2 | **People** | *Who* is it for? | *Tribes*, *We Are All Weird* |
| 3 | **Product** | *What* is remarkable enough to be worth talking about? | *Purple Cow*, *Free Prize Inside*, *Meatball Sundae* |
| 4 | **Permission** | *How* do we enter their attention, without interrupting? | *Permission Marketing* |
| 5 | **Promise** | *What* story do we tell that they'll want to retell? | *All Marketers Are Liars / Tell Stories* |
| 6 | **Propagation** | *How* does the idea spread on its own? | *Unleashing the Ideavirus*, *Small is the New Big* |
| 7 | **Persistence** | *How* do we sustain the work — ship, push through, matter? | *The Practice*, *The Dip*, *Linchpin*, *Poke the Box* |

### The one-line arc
> Find a change worth making **(Purpose)** → for a specific tribe **(People)** → with a remarkable offering **(Product)** → earn their attention **(Permission)** → through a story they want to tell **(Promise)** → engineered to spread **(Propagation)** → shipped relentlessly **(Persistence)**.

## 1. Purpose — "Marketing is the change you seek to make"

### Godin's principle
- Don't start with "how do I sell more?" Start with: *Who is it for? What change do I seek to make?*
- Marketing is generosity in service of transformation. It is not advertising.
- If you can't name the change in one sentence, you don't have a strategy.
- From the blog: *"People don't want what you make. They want what it will do for them. They want the way it will make them feel."*

### Applied to Kompete.ai
**The change we seek to make:**
> Turn competitive intelligence from a $50K/year luxury for a dedicated PMM into an autonomous, actionable system every revenue team uses — so B2B companies stop losing deals, customers, and months of pipeline to competitor moves they didn't see.

**What we're doing right**
- We've named the change clearly: CI should be autonomous, actionable, accessible — not a dashboard PMM maintains.
- The war room doc leads with a generous, buyer-centric frame ("the person who loses money when CI fails") rather than a feature brag.
- The 27 interviews are generosity-shaped research: we listened before we built.

**What's still open**
- The homepage hero on most variants still reads like a *product description* ("AI-native competitive intelligence"), not a *change statement* ("Stop losing deals to competitor moves you never saw"). Lead with the transformation.
- We haven't drawn a clean line between *"competitive intelligence platform"* and *"competitive revenue engine"* — the latter is the change, the former is the category. Godin would pick the change.

## 2. People — "Serve the smallest viable audience"

### Godin's principle
- The mass market is dead. Find a tribe — a group connected by an idea, a leader, and each other.
- Don't chase everyone; delight the specific few. They will bring the rest.
- Tribes want to be led, not herded. Leadership > management.
- From the blog: *"People like us do things like this."* — this sentence is the entire book of positioning.
- **Watch-out (April 2026):** *"One can earn more status by caring more about the issue that others are adjacent to. And so the loop gains momentum… But when we focus on purity and status at the expense of the journey, the distraction costs all of us."* — [Avoiding the purity loop, Apr 13 2026](https://seths.blog/2026/04/avoiding-the-purity-loop/). Tribes can become purity loops where members compete on stricter stances instead of shared work. Lead the tribe; don't let it eat itself.

### Applied to Kompete.ai
**Our smallest viable audience (the first tribe):**
Founders and CMOs at $5M–$50M ARR B2B SaaS who are personally spending 10 hrs/week on competitor research, don't have a PMM, and have already tried stitching together Perplexity + Semrush + manual tracking.

**What we're doing right**
- The ICP is sharp in the war room: $5M–$200M ARR, 5–25 competitors, US-first.
- We've resisted the "for everyone" trap — interviews explicitly call out enterprise (SAP/Oracle-class) as *not our ICP*.
- The buyer persona list is ordered by budget clarity, not by enthusiasm. That's MECE discipline.

**What's still open**
- The homepage variants try to speak to Sales, Marketing, Product, CS, Strategy, and Founders all at once. Six audiences = no audience. Pick *one* tribe for the hero, let the product tabs serve the rest.
- The phrase "people like us" hasn't been crystallized. Who is the Kompete user, identity-wise? ("Operators who refuse to be surprised." / "Founders who compete on speed of response.") This belongs on the site and in every piece of content.
- We don't yet have a named tribe ritual — a weekly digest subscribers become part of, a Slack, a "State of Competition" report. Tribes need a meeting place.

## 3. Product — "Be remarkable (the Purple Cow)"

### Godin's principle
- Boring is invisible. "Very good" is the new average, and average is a death sentence.
- Build remarkability *into* the product — don't bolt it on with advertising afterward.
- The product *is* the marketing. If it's not worth talking about, no amount of spend will save it.
- From the blog: *"Safe is risky."* The only safe move is to be unmistakable.
- **Recent (April 2026):** *"Every writer has all of them. 26 in most Western languages. But no writer knows all the words… don't spend a lot of time admiring your letters. It's the words that matter."* — [All the letters, Apr 7 2026](https://seths.blog/2026/04/all-the-letters/). Everyone has the same AI building blocks now. The remarkable thing isn't the inputs (LLMs, scrapers, dashboards) — it's the *combination*. Kompete's "letters" are commodity; the *word* we spell with them is the moat.

### Applied to Kompete.ai
**What's genuinely remarkable (the Purple Cows):**
1. **Sub-3-minute alerts** vs. Klue/Crayon's daily digests. An order-of-magnitude improvement.
2. **Strategy Timeline** — a competitive system of record no one else has. The longer you use it, the more valuable it gets.
3. **Claim verification** — cross-referencing competitor claims against reviews, outages, benchmarks. Nobody else does this.
4. **AEO tracking** — first-mover on how competitors rank in ChatGPT/Perplexity/Gemini. No incumbent offers this.
5. **5-minute setup** vs. weeks/months for legacy CI.
6. **Team-specific actions**, not data dumps — each team gets *what to do*, not *what happened*.

**What we're doing right**
- Several features are genuine Purple Cows, not "also better." AEO, Claim Audit, Strategy Timeline could each anchor a category.
- Pricing is itself remarkable — "free early access, fraction of legacy" is a talking point.
- The "5 minutes vs. weeks" setup time is a story people retell without being asked.

**What's still open**
- The site currently buries the remarkable features inside a 30-tab feature tour. Godin would argue: **pick one Purple Cow and lead with it**. Every remarkable feature beyond the first dilutes the one thing people will remember.
- We haven't picked a "Free Prize Inside" — the small, delightful surprise that isn't the main event but makes people talk. (E.g., the first report you see being about *your own company* scraped live — "here's what your competitors will see about you.")
- The product doesn't yet have a signature visual/verbal tic the way Purple Cow products do (think Mailchimp's chimp, Notion's aesthetic, Stripe's clean IA). Our design language on v6.2 is strong but not yet distinctive.

## 4. Permission — "Anticipated, personal, and relevant"

### Godin's principle
- Interruption marketing is bankrupt. Attention is the scarcest resource.
- Permission = anticipated (they expect to hear from you), personal (it's for them), relevant (it matters).
- The asset is not the email list. The asset is the *privilege of being expected*.
- From the blog: *"Treating people with respect is the best way to earn their attention."*
- **Recent (April 2026):** *"These are spammers disguised as door-to-door salespeople… Burning trust to get attention rarely pays off."* — [Attention and effort, Apr 9 2026](https://seths.blog/2026/04/attention-and-effort/). In a world where AI agents are flooding inboxes with personalized-but-spammy outreach, a CI tool that *verifies competitor claims* against evidence is selling exactly the right thing: trust. Lean into this in the brand voice — Kompete is the anti-AI-slop tool, even though it's AI-native.
- **Recent (April 2026):** *"If it's just the right amount of necessary kindness, it's not really kindness. It's pleasantness."* — [Kinder than necessary, Apr 6 2026](https://seths.blog/2026/04/kinder-than-necessary/). The free tier, the public newsletter, the State-of-CI report — these aren't marketing. They're *kinder than necessary*, which is the only marketing that compounds.

### Applied to Kompete.ai
**What we're doing right**
- "Free early access" is a permission-first offer — we're trading value for attention, not demanding attention for a demo.
- The research process itself built permission: 27 operators invested real time in interviews, which means a warm community primed to hear from us.
- Team-specific weekly briefs (the product itself) are a permission asset — people opt in to hear from us, anticipate it, find it relevant.

**What's still open**
- We don't yet run a **public weekly competitive newsletter** ("This week in B2B CI: 7 moves worth knowing"). This is the highest-leverage permission asset we could build. It's also a marketing-for-the-product-with-the-product move — eat our own alerts.
- No anonymous "State of Competition" report, no quarterly benchmark — these are classic Godin-style gift-first assets that earn the right to follow up.
- The site has no clear permission ladder: *reader → subscriber → trial user → customer*. Right now it jumps from *reader* to *book a demo*.

## 5. Promise — "Tell a true story that resonates"

### Godin's principle
- People don't buy features; they buy stories that match the worldview they already hold.
- The story must be authentic — lived all the way through the product, team, and experience — or it collapses.
- Frame the story around *their* identity ("people like us do things like this"), not your specs.
- From the blog: *"Marketers don't make things. They make meaning."*
- **Recent (April 2026):** *"The right answer doesn't care about how you feel. It's still the right answer… Feelings have a huge evolutionary head start on facts."* — [The right answer, Apr 6 2026](https://seths.blog/2026/04/the-right-answer-2/). Kompete has a rare B2B asset: we can tell both stories at once. Our *feeling* story is "never be caught off-guard again." Our *fact* story is the claim-verification engine and 3-minute alerts. Leading with feeling and closing with facts is the highest-leverage narrative structure for us.

### Applied to Kompete.ai
**The stories we can tell (ranked by resonance):**

| Story | Worldview it confirms | Who it's for |
|-------|----------------------|--------------|
| "Stop finding out about competitor moves from your prospect." | *Being caught off-guard is unprofessional* | Sales leaders, founders |
| "Your battle cards are stale within a week. Mine update themselves." | *Manual work is a tax on my team* | PMM, Marketing |
| "CI shouldn't cost $50K/yr and a dedicated headcount." | *Legacy enterprise pricing is lazy* | Founders, CFOs |
| "Perplexity answers what you asked. Kompete answers what you didn't know to ask." | *I don't know what I don't know, and that terrifies me* | Everyone |
| "Competitors leave fingerprints every day. We catch them in 3 minutes." | *Speed is the moat now* | Sales, CEOs |

**What we're doing right**
- The "Cost of a Blind Spot" timeline stories (A–K in `competitive-comparison-stories.md`) are classic Godinesque narrative — concrete trigger, stepwise consequence, emotional arc, dollar bottom-line.
- The *Without / With* structure is a story people retell *on their own*, which is the real test.
- Every story is anchored in a true customer quote — zero fabrication, high credibility.

**What's still open**
- The site doesn't lead with *one* canonical story — it offers a menu. Godin: pick one story and tell it brilliantly. The homepage should have a single "Tuesday, 3:15 PM" hero scene, not a story buffet.
- We haven't crystallized the *identity* story for the buyer: "I'm the kind of operator who refuses to compete blind." This is the sentence customers should say *to themselves* while reading the site.
- No founder story yet. The most durable B2B narratives start with a personal origin ("I spent 10 hours a week on competitor research and it was still incomplete, so I built the thing I wished existed.") We have the raw material; we haven't told it publicly.

## 6. Propagation — "Design ideas that spread (the Ideavirus)"

### Godin's principle
- In a connected world, ideas with built-in velocity win. Marketers no longer distribute — they infect.
- Make it easy, worth talking about, and *status-enhancing* to share.
- Sneezers (connectors) matter more than audiences. Find the 12 people who'll tell 100 people each.
- From the blog: *"The cow, by definition, is remarkable. Something remarkable is worth making a remark about."*

### Applied to Kompete.ai
**What we're doing right**
- The product itself has viral seams: auto-generated comparison pages, shareable alerts, team-specific briefs — each one is a distribution channel.
- "Comparison pages drive MQLs" is literally the customer's observation, and our Feature Matrix builds them automatically. The product spreads the product.
- Free early access lowers the bar to sharing ("I got into a thing, you should try it too").

**What's still open**
- No **public, shareable "competitive teardown"** asset. E.g., "We ran Kompete against Klue on Klue — here's what it found." This is an ideavirus waiting to happen in PMM circles.
- No embeddable or screenshot-friendly moments built into the product. Godin-era playbook: make it frictionless to post a Kompete screenshot to LinkedIn and have it look great. Every tweet is a billboard.
- No sneezer list. Who are the 30 PMMs, 20 sales leaders, and 10 CEOs whose endorsement would move the market? We should know them by name and be building relationships with them — this is *Tribes* work.
- The "State of Competition" annual report is the single highest-propagation asset we're not yet building.

## 7. Persistence — "Ship the practice; push through the Dip"

### Godin's principle
- The work isn't a campaign; it's a *practice*. Ship consistently, without waiting for a muse. (*The Practice*)
- Every worthwhile endeavor has a Dip — the long slog between excitement and mastery. Quit the cul-de-sacs; lean into the Dips that lead somewhere. (*The Dip*)
- Be a Linchpin: do emotional labor, take the initiative, make art. Indispensability is the only durable moat. (*Linchpin*)
- Start. Ship. Poke the box. (*Poke the Box*)
- From the blog: *"This might not work. That's the whole point."*
- **Recent (April 2026):** *"If you're hoping for this meeting or this performance or this engagement to produce something extraordinary, why are you setting it up as if it's ordinary?… If it doesn't feel like you're putting a lot of effort into creating the conditions for magic, you're probably not creating those conditions."* — [Creating the conditions for magic, Apr 10 2026](https://seths.blog/2026/04/creating-the-conditions-for-magic/). Applies directly to every demo, investor pitch, and customer onboarding. The demo isn't magic by accident — it's staged. Every Kompete first-run experience should feel engineered for magic, not assembled.

### Applied to Kompete.ai
**What we're doing right**
- The dated website snapshots (`snapshots/YYYY-MM-DD/`) are literally a shipping practice — we can see the velocity of iteration.
- v3 → v4 → v6.2 in under two months is Godin-grade shipping cadence.
- The war room is a Linchpin artifact — a living document instead of a frozen deck, updated after every conversation.

**What's still open**
- The Dip we're in: the gap between "we have a remarkable product" and "operators in our ICP know about it." This is exactly the Dip worth leaning into — not quitting, but also not pretending we're past it. Specifically: the next 90–180 days of evangelism is the Dip. Expect it to feel slow.
- No public shipping cadence yet. A weekly or bi-weekly "what shipped at Kompete.ai" post — public changelog as permission-and-propagation asset.
- The practice of *writing daily* (Godin's own most-cited habit) isn't in place for the team. A founder blog, even 200 words a day, compounds harder than any paid channel at our stage.

## Appendix A — Godin Blog Themes Worth Stealing

Recurring ideas in his blog that compress his books into reusable moves:

- **"Is it for us?"** — the three-word test for any marketing asset.
- **"The smallest viable audience"** — always smaller than you think.
- **"Trust is the new currency"** — in an AI-slop world, this is asymmetrically valuable for a CI product that verifies claims.
- **"Status roles"** — why people share: dominance, affiliation, belonging. Every Kompete share should answer *"what status does sharing this confer?"*
- **"Tension, not hype"** — marketing that creates tension (a question the reader needs resolved) outperforms marketing that shouts.
- **"Ship the work, not the art"** — done is the engine of better.
- **"Do it again tomorrow"** — the practice compounds; campaigns decay.

## Appendix B — Recent Blog Posts (April 2026), Cited & Applied

A fresh sample of Seth's writing from the past two weeks, mapped to the 7 principles and to concrete Kompete.ai moves.

### 1. [Attention and effort](https://seths.blog/2026/04/attention-and-effort/) — Apr 9, 2026
**Principle:** Permission (and Trust)
**Quote:** *"These are spammers disguised as door-to-door salespeople… Our instinct is to respect the work of a pitch that took more effort to create than it will cost us to consume… Burning trust to get attention rarely pays off."*
**Application to Kompete.ai:**
- The B2B outbound world is about to be drowned in AI-generated "personalized" sequences. Our brand asset is the opposite: a product that **verifies** claims against evidence. Lean into "the anti-slop CI tool" as a voice.
- Every outreach *we* send should feel human and effortful — not volume. A 30-operator warm outreach list beats a 3,000-person cold sequence.
- Pricing signal: the fact that Kompete puts effort into each alert (claim verification, team-specific action) is itself a trust move. Surface it.

### 2. [Creating the conditions for magic](https://seths.blog/2026/04/creating-the-conditions-for-magic/) — Apr 10, 2026
**Principle:** Persistence (and Product — first-run magic)
**Quote:** *"If you're hoping for this meeting or this performance or this engagement to produce something extraordinary, why are you setting it up as if it's ordinary?… We hire architects to design expensive buildings, but we design expensive human interactions as an afterthought."*
**Application to Kompete.ai:**
- The **first-run demo** is our highest-leverage interaction. It should not be a generic tour. The visitor should see their *own* competitive landscape scraped live within 60 seconds.
- Investor conversations get the same treatment — script the "magic moment" (e.g., live competitor alert during the call), don't hope for it.
- Every Friday ship day should include one "conditions for magic" item, not just features.

### 3. [All the letters](https://seths.blog/2026/04/all-the-letters/) — Apr 7, 2026
**Principle:** Product (remarkable = novel combinations, not novel inputs)
**Quote:** *"Every writer has all of them. 26 in most Western languages. But no writer knows all the words… don't spend a lot of time admiring your letters. It's the words that matter."*
**Application to Kompete.ai:**
- Every CI tool now has access to the same LLMs, the same scrapers, the same review APIs. The letters are commodity.
- Our "word" — the unique combination — is: *system-of-record history* + *sub-3-min alerts* + *team-specific actions* + *claim verification* + *AEO*. No competitor has this sentence.
- Stop pitching the letters (AI! Real-time! Comprehensive!). Pitch the word only we can spell.

### 4. [Avoiding the purity loop](https://seths.blog/2026/04/avoiding-the-purity-loop/) — Apr 13, 2026
**Principle:** People (Tribe leadership, watch-out)
**Quote:** *"One can earn more status by caring more about the issue that others are adjacent to. And so the loop gains momentum… But when we focus on purity and status at the expense of the journey, the distraction costs all of us."*
**Application to Kompete.ai:**
- As we build the CI tribe (newsletter, community, content), resist the purity-loop trap — don't let discourse drift into "real CI professionals don't use Perplexity / don't buy Klue / don't do X."
- Lead with shared work and shared outcomes (*"we refuse to compete blind"*), not with shared enemies or shared purity tests.
- Practical check: any content we publish that scores points by making competitors' customers feel stupid is a purity-loop signal. Kill it.

### 5. [Kinder than necessary](https://seths.blog/2026/04/kinder-than-necessary/) — Apr 6, 2026
**Principle:** Permission + Promise
**Quote:** *"If it's just the right amount of necessary kindness, it's not really kindness. It's pleasantness."*
**Application to Kompete.ai:**
- "Kinder than necessary" is a product design principle too. Free early access isn't marketing — it's a kindness. The weekly public CI newsletter (even to people who'll never buy) is a kindness.
- Anything that feels transactionally "just enough" — a gated report, a demo-walled product tour, a grudging freemium — falls short of the standard.
- Name the standard internally: *"Is this kinder than necessary?"* should be a question in every launch review.

### 6. [The right answer](https://seths.blog/2026/04/the-right-answer-2/) — Apr 6, 2026
**Principle:** Promise (narrative structure)
**Quote:** *"The right answer doesn't care about how you feel. It's still the right answer… Feelings have a huge evolutionary head start on facts."*
**Application to Kompete.ai:**
- Most B2B CI competitors lead with facts (features, coverage, accuracy). We can lead with feeling (*"never be caught off-guard again"*) and close with facts (claim-verification, 3-min alerts, evidence cross-referencing).
- For the homepage: the hero should land a feeling in 5 seconds; the proof-stack (timeline stories, feature matrix, customer quotes) should land the facts in the next 30 seconds.
- For sales: open the deck with the feeling story (one of Stories A–K from `competitive-comparison-stories.md`), not the feature tour.

### 7. [Settling](https://seths.blog/2026/04/settling/) — Apr 12, 2026
**Principle:** Purpose (discipline around the change you seek)
**Summary:** Distinguishes between accepting what you receive and compromising your standards to get something.
**Application to Kompete.ai:**
- Don't settle on positioning. If "CI platform" is the category we reluctantly accept because buyers search for it, fine — but our *narrative* should be "competitive revenue engine" or whatever tells the truer story.
- Applies to ICP discipline too: every time we're tempted to close a deal outside our sweet spot ($5–200M B2B SaaS), we're settling in a way that costs us for months.

### 8. [Plumbed](https://seths.blog/2026/04/plumbed/) — Apr 5, 2026
**Principle:** Product (design for the path of least friction)
**Summary:** Convenience drives consumption — what's plumbed-in gets used, what isn't doesn't.
**Application to Kompete.ai:**
- Adoption = plumbing. Battle cards in Salesforce, alerts in Slack, briefs in email — these aren't integrations, they're plumbing. If CI isn't plumbed into where the team already works, it won't be used. This is already in the product roadmap; this post reinforces it as a top-level priority, not a "nice to have."

## How to use this doc
- Before any marketing decision (headline, page, launch, ad), ask the 7 questions in order.
- If we can't answer **Purpose** and **People** in one sentence each, stop and fix that first. The rest won't work without them.
- Re-visit this doc quarterly. The MECE frame is stable; the applied layer evolves as the product and tribe evolve.
