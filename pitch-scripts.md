## How to use this doc

1. **Default to the Plain Pitch.** It works for anyone — no analogy required, doesn't over-sell. Use unless audience clearly fits a specialized variant.
2. **Switch to an analogy version** only when the audience archetype is clear (Bloomberg for finance/investors, Truecaller for Indian operators, cricket/Waze for casual).
3. **Pick the length** — 60s, 30s, 10s, or the 3-second meme — based on the setting.
4. **Pre-think the Q&A** — the questions in the bank below are the ones we get most often.
5. **Close with a status hook**, not enthusiasm.
6. **Never read this verbatim.** Internalize the structure, then deliver in your own voice.

**Things to AVOID:**
- Don't lead with "AI" or "scraping" or "real-time" — those are infrastructure, not value
- Don't promise sub-3-minute alerts — operationally we're a daily brief, occasional real-time
- Don't pitch to a room — pitch to one person at a time who fits the profile
- Don't say "competitive intelligence" early — say it after they're hooked

## ⭐ The Default Pitch (Plain — no analogy, no over-sell)

The simplest possible version. Works for anyone — Indian, Western, founder, investor, friend, family. Doesn't require knowing what Bloomberg, Truecaller, or Waze is. Doesn't over-sell. Just describes the product so plainly that the listener gets it the first time.

**Use this as the default.** Switch to an analogy version only when the audience clearly maps to one.

### 60-second version

> "You know how you usually find out about a competitor's move from a customer mentioning it on a sales call — and by then the deal's already slipping?
>
> Kompete fixes that. Every morning, your team gets one short email: what every competitor did yesterday, what it means for you, and what to do about it. Sales, marketing, product — each gets the part that matters to them.
>
> That's the whole product. No dashboards to log into, no spreadsheets to maintain, no analyst to hire. Just a daily brief that keeps you ahead instead of behind.
>
> We're hand-picking the first 100 founders for early access right now. Want in?"

### 30-second version

> "Most B2B teams find out about competitor moves from a customer on a call — by then it's too late. Kompete fixes that. Every morning, one email to your team: what every competitor did, what it means for you, what to do about it. Sales, marketing, product — each gets the part that matters. Hand-picking the first 100 founders. Want in?"

### 10-second version

> "Every morning, one email to your team — what your competitors did, what it means, what to do. That's it."

### 3-second meme (the line that travels)

> **"A daily competitor brief — for your whole team."**

Alternative meme (use whichever lands better in conversation):

> **"Stop finding out about competitors from your customers."**

### Why this works (the honest version)

- **No analogy needed.** The product describes itself in 7 words.
- **No over-selling.** "That's the whole product" is a Godin-style under-promise that builds trust.
- **The pain is universal and embarrassing.** Every operator has been in the "found out from a customer" moment.
- **The cadence is honest** — daily morning brief, not "real-time AI alerts." Operationally deliverable.
- **The 3 things are folded in** ("what they did, what it means, what to do") — same anchors as the website, said in plain English.
- **The status close is light** — "first 100 founders" is enough scarcity, not desperate.

One intelligent brief every morning — what every competitor did, what it means, what your team should do. So you stop being the bottleneck. Bloomberg-style synthesis, AI-native, fraction of Klue's price. Want early access?"

**Why this works:** Hits a pain they personally feel. Cold Start hard side play — founders are who pull the rest of the org in.

### 💼 VP Marketing / CMO

> "Your comparison pages are the #1 driver of MQLs in B2B SaaS — and they go stale within a week of every competitor change. Kompete watches positioning, SEO, content, paid channels, AND AI search across ChatGPT and Perplexity. One morning brief tells your team what changed, what it means for your messaging, and what to update. Klue does battle cards manually for $50K. We do all of it AI-native."

**Why this works:** Lands on revenue impact (MQLs) and adds the AEO wedge (no incumbent has it).

### 💰 Head of Sales / CRO

> "Your reps walk into competitive deals blind. They find out about competitor pricing changes from the prospect — and lose deals they didn't know they were competing for. Kompete gives every rep a living battle card: what competitors did this week, what it means for active deals, what to say. In their CRM. Without a PMM maintaining it."

**Why this works:** Speaks Sales' language (deals, reps, win rate). Doesn't mention "intelligence" — sales doesn't care about intel, they care about closing.

### 🛠️ VP Product / CPO

> "Your roadmap decisions cost millions. Right now you're making them on three-month-old competitor data. ## Q&A Bank — what to say when they push

Memorize these. They cover ~90% of follow-up questions.

### "How does it actually work?"

> "Three things. **One** — it monitors every public signal a competitor leaves: pricing pages, G2/Capterra reviews, LinkedIn, hiring, ads, blog, social, news. **Two** — it cross-references claims against evidence, so you're not getting raw data, you're getting verified intelligence. **Three** — it routes to the right team with the right action: sales gets battle cards, marketing gets counter-positioning, product gets gap analysis, founder gets the weekly brief. It's not a dashboard, it's an autopilot."

### "How is this different from ChatGPT / Perplexity / Deep Research?"

> "ChatGPT is a search engine — it answers what you ask, has no memory, no monitoring, no system of record. Kompete is a system — it monitors continuously, builds a 12-month historical timeline, learns your competitive landscape, and proactively delivers intelligence to every team. You'd have to run 75 ChatGPT prompts a day to get what we deliver in one morning brief."

### "How is this different from Klue / Crayon?"

> "Klue and Crayon were built before AI. They give you battle cards but you still maintain them manually — 14 of 27 PMMs we interviewed said their battle cards go stale within a week. Kompete updates itself. They cost $40–80K/year and need a full-time analyst. We're AI-native, fraction of the cost, zero headcount. Same job, autonomous."

### "What does it cost?"

> "Free for the first 100 early-access customers. After that, low subscription with usage-based pricing. Roughly $3–5K/year for a typical team — vs. Klue's $40–80K. The economics aren't even close."

### "Who's it for?"

> "Founders and CMOs at $5–50M ARR B2B SaaS who have 5–25 competitors and are personally tracking them today. If you've ever found out about a competitor's move from a customer mention, you're our exact buyer."

### "What's the moat? Won't this be commoditized?"

> "Two things. **One** — the **Strategy Timeline**. We build a 12-month historical record of every competitor move; the longer you use it, the more pattern recognition we accumulate, the harder it is to switch. **Two** — **Claim Verification**. We're the only tool that cross-references every competitor claim against evidence. That's a hard technical moat. Anyone can scrape today's data; nobody else has the history layer or the verification layer."

### "Why now?"

> "Two reasons. **One** — AI has finally reached the point where you can automate the *entire* CI workflow: monitoring, analysis, synthesis, and recommendation. Legacy CI tools were built before this was possible. **Two** — competitors are moving faster than ever (AI shortened every product cycle), so the cost of being blindsided has gone up dramatically. The window to win this category is now."

### "How accurate is it? What about hallucinations?"

> "Every signal in our brief is sourced and verifiable — you click any insight and see the underlying evidence. We don't generate insights without source documents. And our Claim Verification layer cross-references competitor claims against G2 reviews, outage data, and benchmarks — so we'll actually flag when a competitor's claim contradicts public evidence."

### "Can I just hire an intern to do this?"

> "Sure, you could pay an intern $40K to manually check 30 sources every day and write a Slack post. But by week 3 they're missing things, by week 6 they're doing other work too, and you have no historical record when they leave. Kompete is the same job, automated, with a 12-month memory and zero turnover."

## Delivery notes — how to land any of these

1. **Pause after the analogy.** Whether it's "Bloomberg Terminal," "Truecaller," "Waze," or "cricket scoreboard" — let the metaphor land before you bridge to competitors. People need a beat.
2. **Don't say "AI" too early.** Say "watches" or "synthesizes" first. "AI" comes after they're already curious — otherwise it's category fatigue.
3. **The "$50K vs us" line is doing real work.** It frames Klue as expensive *and* worse, while signaling we're the new infrastructure. Don't skip it.
4. **End with scarcity, not enthusiasm.** "Hand-picking the first 100 founders" >>> "It's amazing, you'll love it." (Cold Start: status > information.)
5. **The 3-second meme is the test.** If your listener walks away and tells one person, they'll say *"Bloomberg / Truecaller / Waze — but for competitors."* That's the only thing that has to survive intact. Everything else is supporting cast.
6. **Pitch one-on-one.** Stage 1 (Cold Start) propagation works through 1:1 hand-recruitment, not rooms or broadcasts.

## Anti-patterns — what NOT to say

- ❌ **"It scrapes the internet"** — sounds technical and cheap. Say "watches" or "monitors."
- ❌ **"Real-time, sub-3-minute alerts"** — overpromises, and speed isn't actually our differentiator. Synthesis + impact + decisions is.
- ❌ **"It's an AI agent"** — the product is module-based, not agent-based. Stay truthful (Godin Principle #5: the story has to be lived through the product).
- ❌ **"It's like ChatGPT for competitors"** — undersells us; ChatGPT has no memory or system of record.
- ❌ **"Competitive Intelligence Platform"** — category language, not change language. Use "Bloomberg of competitive intelligence" or "competitive revenue engine."
- ❌ **"It does everything"** — sounds desperate. Stick to the 3 anchors.
- ❌ **Pitching to a room of mixed personas** — water down for everyone = land for no one. Pick one persona, pitch to one person.

## Why these scripts work — Godin + Chen layered

| Element | Source | Why it's there |
|---|---|---|
| Bloomberg / Truecaller / Waze openers | Godin: *true story matching a worldview* | Listener has a built-in model for this; we just bridge to a new use case |
| The 3 anchors (synthesis / impact / action) | Godin: *Promise* + Kompete website copy | Specific, true, on-product — story can be lived through to the product |
| "$50K Klue" contrast | Godin: *positioning by negation* | Frames the incumbent as the expensive, broken option |
| "Hand-picking first 100" | Chen: *atomic network* + status | Scarcity + insider feel = they tell others unprompted |
| 3-second meme at the end | Chen: *word-of-mouth design* | Survives the telephone game; can be repeated verbatim |
| Pause after the analogy | Godin: *tension over hype* | Listener pulls the next question instead of being pushed info |
| Persona-specific openers | Chen: *hard side recruitment* | Different doorways for different "hard side" personas (founders, CMOs) |
| Q&A bank for objections | Chen: *magic moment design* | Removing friction from the "oh shit, I want this" moment |
